Introduction to eGov Property Tax Information System

City Corporation is the major service-providing institution to the public. According to the 74th amendment of the Constitution, it is obliged to provide basic services and infrastructure such as health, water supply, and basic amenities to citizens. In order to carry out these functions, City Corporation require adequate revenue. Property tax is the major source of its revenue, as the tax power is vested in the City Corporation to specify the valuation method, rate band, and collection procedures.


It is significant to collect the property tax from citizens on time to ensure that the demands of City Corporation in terms of providing service delivery to the public are met on a timely manner.


As a result of the increase in urban population and the number of property owners, the same old pattern of tax collection is not effective and does not yield substantial revenue to the City Corporation.

 

Property Tax Information System (PTIS) is an application, exclusively designed and developed for the City Corporation, to ensure that the collection of the property tax is optimized. The application also brings about transparency in tax calculation.

 

Key Features


The key features of the application are:

·             Creation of a Property: When the field officer discovers a new property or if the citizen informs the Corporation about a new property, you can register a new property in the system with minimal effort and no supervision.

·             Modification of a Property: When the field officer discovers a change in the property details or if the citizen informs the Corporation about any change in the property details, you can update the details in the system.

·             Amalgamation: When two or more properties are merged to form a single property, you can make sure that the information is reflected in the system by performing a few simple steps. The system automatically deactivates the individual parent properties and creates a new merged property.

·             Bifurcation: When a citizen wants to divide a property to form multiple individual properties, you are required to update the information in the system. You can do this task in a very little time using the PTIS system.

·             Transfer: You can transfer the ownership (rights) of a property from one person to another.

·             Tax Computation: Computation of tax is a complex process, and it depends on various factors such as the area, location, type, and occupancy of the property. Therefore, change in any of these factors will result in increase/decrease in the tax amount. The system keeps track of every change that happens to a property and automatically computes the exact tax accordingly for every property that is registered.

·             Reports: This feature gives the overall status of the properties registered and the tax details in a tabular form. This allows easy access to the notices generated earlier and allows to print.

·             Approval cycles to ensure the integrity of the data: A property has to be approved after it is registered or transferred to another person. The application ensures that each property undergoes a series of approval before any action is done on it.
