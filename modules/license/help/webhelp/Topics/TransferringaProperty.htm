Transferring Ownership of a Property

Previous Top Next 

You can transfer the ownership of a registered property from one owner to another due to the following reasons:

·      Inheritance: It is the practice of passing on the ownership of the property to another individual/institution upon the death of the original owner.

·      Sale: When a person sells a property, then the ownership of the property must be transferred to whom the property is sold.

·      Gift: When a person gifts a property, the ownership of the property must be transferred to the person to whom the property is gifted.

·      Settlement: The ownership of a disputed property must be transferred to the individual who is declared to be the owner by the court or by personal compromise between the two individuals.

·      Will: When an individual inherits a property through Will, you must transfer the ownership of the property to that individual.

·      Clerical error: When the name of the property owner is wrongly typed (wrong or misspelt name) because of some clerical errors, you can transfer the property to the correct name.

·      Court Order: When the ownership of a property is declared through a court order, you must transfer the ownership of the property to the concerned individual.

·      Family Arrangement: When there is no will written, then the head of the family distributes the property amongst the family members.

·      Partition Deed: The owner decides about the division and distribution of the property amongst the heirs.

·      Allotment: The government departments allot plots/flats to their employees.

·      Hibba: This is a special case of transfer of property in the Muslim community. The mosque authority donates the land/house to a less privileged individual.

·      Release Deed: It is a legal document in which one who has limited rights to a part of property abandons those rights back to the owner of the property.

·      Gazette Notification: When an individual legally changes the name, you must transfer the ownership of the property to the new name.

·      Amalgamation of Companies: When two or more companies merge together, the properties owned by the subsidiary companies must be transferred to the name of the merged company.

 

NOTE: You must transfer the ownership of a property to another individual only if the individual submits necessary documents that support the transfer.


To transfer ownership of a property, perform the following steps:

1.   Find the property that you want to transfer the ownership of.

2.   From the Actions drop-down list, select Transfer Ownerships to display the Transfer of Property OwnerShip page.

3.   In the Application Number field, type the application number. This number is given on the Transfer of Ownership Form submitted by the citizen.

4.   In the Application Date field, type the date on which the form is submitted.

5.   From the Reason for Transfer drop-down list, select one of the options as described above.

6.   Specify the following property details:

Table 1: Transfer of Property Ownership

Field

Description

Sale Consideration (Applicable in case of Sale Deed)

The amount in rupees paid by the individual who buys the property.

Document No.

A unique number given by the Sub-Registrar to the new owner of the property.

Document Date

The date on which the property is transferred in the name of the new owner.

NOTE: This date has to be earlier than the Application Date.

Sub Registrar Office Name

The name of the Sub-Registrar office where the ownership transfer occurred.

North By

The door number of the construction to the north of the property.

East By

The door number of the construction to the east of the property.

South By

The door number of the construction to the south of the property.

West By

 

The door number of the construction to the west of the property.

Owner Name           

The name of the property owner(s) to whom the property is transferred.

NOTE: If there are multiple owners, click the Plus (+) icon and type the name of the additional owner(s). Similarly, click the Cross (X) icon to delete the name of an owner.

Mobile No

The mobile phone number of the new owner.

E-mail Address

The e-mail address of the new owner.

Correspondence Address


 

            

 

The address of correspondence between the new property owner and the corporation.

NOTE: You need to select the Correspondence Address check box to type the details.

 

7.   Click Transfer Property  to save the changes (or click Back to go to the previous page). A Transfer of Property Acknowledgement page is displayed.

8.   Click one of the following options: 

·    Confirm to confirm the details you have entered.

·    Back to modify the details you have entered.

  

NOTE: Upon clicking Confirm, you get an acknowledgement page with the following information:

·  Bill number of the property

·  Assessee name

·  Name of the new owner

·  Address of the property

 

Along with the acknowledgement, you get the Notice No.9 with the property tax details. You can choose to print this notice.
