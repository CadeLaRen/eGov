Creating a New Property

Previous Top Next 

You can register a new property when:

·      the assessor discovers a new unassessed property during field survey and gets the correct property details.

·      citizens inform the corporation about a property which is yet to be registered in their names.

·      if the property details are not available in the PTIS system a citizen can also create a new property. To check the availability of the property details in the PTIS system, see Finding a Property.

 

To register a new property, perform the following steps:

1.  Click Property Tax from the left navigation bar.

2.  Click New Property.

3.  Click Form 6 Create New Property Workflow to display the Create Property: Form-6  page.

4.  From the Reason for Creation of Property drop-down list, select NEW PROPERTY.

5.  Specify the following property details:

Table 1: Create Property

Field

Description

Zone

The zone to which the property belongs.

Ward

The ward to which the property belongs.

Owner Name

The name of the property owner(s). 


NOTE: 

·       If the property has multiple owners, click the Plus (+) icon and type the name of the additional owner(s). Similarly, click the Cross (X) icon to delete the selected owner.

 

·      You must type the name of the owner exactly as it is given in the form submitted. Once you register the property with a wrong name, you cannot change it. 

 

Mobile Number

The mobile phone number of the property owner.

E-mail Address

The e-mail address of the property owner.

 

6. In the Property Address section, specify the following property details:

Table 2: Property Address

Field

Description

Area

The area to which the property belongs.

Locality

The locality to which the property belongs. 

 

NOTE: The locality names are populated based on the area selected.

 

Street Ward

The ward number that is associated with the street to which the property belongs.

Street Name

The name of the street to which the property belongs.

 

NOTE: The street names are populated based on the street ward selected.

 

Door No. New

The number that is assigned to the property.

 

NOTE: There are two text boxes that are separated by a slash to enter the door number. The second text box is used only if required.

 

Door No.Old

The number that was earlier assigned to the property.

Address 1(Apartment Name/Building Name/Landmark)

Address of the property which includes details such as name of the building, plot number or landmark.

 

NOTE: If the correspondence address is different from the property address, select the If Corresponding Address Different from Property Address check box and enter the details.

 

Pincode

the postal code with first three digits as 600. For example: 600123

7. In the Property Bounded By section, specify the following property details:

Table 3: Property Bounded By

Field

Description

Right

Number of the ward that lies immediately behind the property.

Name of the street that lies immediately behind the property.

Left Street Ward

Number of the ward that lies on the immediate left side of the property.

Left

Name of the street that lies on the immediate left side of the property.

The property number bounded to the north of the property being registered.

South By

The property number bounded to the south of the property being registered.

The property number bounded to the east of the property being registered.

The property number bounded to the west of the property being registered.

 

8. Select the type of the property from the Property Type drop-down list, and perform one of the following steps:

·    If you have selected Super Structure, perform the following steps:

        a. Select one of the following options from the Owner of the Land drop- down list.

·    Temple Land: the land owned by the Temple Authority.

·    Waqf board Land: the land owned by the Waqf Board.

·    Central Govt. Land: the land owned by the Central Government.

·    State Govt Land: the land owned by the State Government.

·    Church Land: the land owned by the Church Authority.

·    Private Land: the land owned by the Private Authority.

·    Chennai Corporation: the land owned by the Corporation of Chennai

·    Others: the property owned by authorities other than the above mentioned list.

       b. In the Land Owner Name field, enter the name of the owner of the land.

       c. In the Floor Details section, specify the details as described in Table 4.


·    If you have selected Vacant Land, perform the following steps:

       a. In the Area of Plot field, enter the total area of the vacant land in square feet.

       b. In the Tax Effective Date field, enter the date (DD/MM/YYYY) effective from when the tax should be calculated.  According to the tax rules, tax cannot be raised with retrospect effect beyond 6 years.

 

NOTE: After you enter the Tax Effective Date, the system automatically displays the installment starting from when you need to collect tax in the Demand Effective Installment field.

           

·    If you have selected Independent Building, perform the following steps:

        a. In the Area of Plot field, enter the total area of the plot where the building stands.

        b. In the Floor Details section, specify the details as mentioned in Table 4.

·    If you have selected Flats in Multistoried Buildings, perform the following steps:

        a. In the Undivided share of Land in Sq.feet field, enter the total area of the plot where the building stands.

        b. In the Floor Details section, specify the details as mentioned in Table 4.

·    If you have selected Central Government 33%, Central Government 75%, or State Government, perform the following steps:

        a. In the Area of Plot field, enter the total area of the plot in square feet.

        b. In the Floor Details section, specify the details as mentioned in Table 4.

·    If you have selected Cell Phone Tower, perform the following steps:

        a. The Cell Phone Tower field is prepopulated with a half-yearly tax amount of Rs. 15,000 and you cannot edit this field.

 

NOTE: The term Cell Phone Towers in the above context refers to the type of property where Base Transceiver Stations (BTS), commonly known as mobile phone towers are installed. For these properties a tax of rupees 15000 has to be collected irrespective of the size and coverage of the BTS.
 

        b. In the Tax Effective Date field, enter the date (DD/MM/YYYY) effective from when the tax should be calculated.  According to the tax rules, tax cannot be raised with retrospect effect beyond 6 years.

 

NOTE: After you enter the Tax Effective Date, the system automatically displays the installment starting from when you need to collect tax in the Demand Effective Installment field.

 

·      If you have selected Cinema Theater, perform the following steps:

a. In the Area of Plot field, enter the total area of the plot where the cinema theater is constructed.

b. From the Category drop-down list, select the category of the cinema theater.

 

NOTE: The cinema theaters are classified as either category A or category B  based on their location and infrastructure. The concerned officer has to go for a site visit and based on the observations classify the cinema theater as either A or B.

 

       c. In the Floor Details section, specify the details as mentioned in Table 4.

                     d. In the Cinema Theater section, specify the following details:

§         Serial No: The serial number

§         Type: The type of the seats in the cinema theater. For example, balcony seats, box seat, gold class, platinum class. It depends on the cinema Theater.

§         No. of Units: The number of seats available for each type.

 

NOTE: If a cinema theater has multiple type of seats, click the Plus (+) icon and enter the details. Similarly, click the Cross (X) icon to delete the selected entry.

 

§         Rate Per Unit: The rate per each seat in rupees.

§         Total: The total amount in rupees. This field automatically gets filled in based on the rate per unit and the number of units.

                 e. In the Date of Completion/Occupation field, enter the date (DD/MM/YYYY) effective from when the tax should be calculated.  According to the tax rules, tax cannot be raised with retrospect effect beyond 6 years.

 

NOTE: After you enter the Date of Completion/Occupation, the system automatically displays the installment starting from when you need to collect tax in the Demand Effective Installment field.

 

·      If you have selected Hospital/Nursing Home, perform the following steps:

a. In the Area of Plot field, enter the total area of the plot where the hospital is constructed

b. From the Category drop-down list, select the category to which the hospital belongs.

 

NOTE: The hospitals are classified as either Government or Private.

 

       c. In the Floor Details section, specify the details as mentioned in Table 4.

                     d. In the Hospital section, specify the following details:

§         Serial No: The serial number

§         Type: The type of wards in the hospital. For example, general, semi duplex, duplex. It depends on the Hospital.

§         No. of Units: The number of rooms available for each type.

 

NOTE: If a hospital has multiple type of wards, click the Plus (+) icon and enter the details. Similarly, click the Cross (X) icon to delete the selected entry.

 

§         Rate Per Unit: The rate per each unit in rupees.

§         Total: The total amount in rupees. This field automatically gets filled in based on the rate per unit and the number of units.

                 e. In the Date of Completion/Occupation field, enter the date (DD/MM/YYYY) effective from when the tax should be calculated.  According to the tax rules, tax cannot be raised with retrospect effect beyond 6 years.

 

NOTE: After you enter the Date of Completion/Occupation, the system automatically displays the installment starting from when you need to collect tax in the Demand Effective Installment field.

 

·      If you have selected  Hotel/Lodging House, perform the following steps:

a. In the Area of Plot field, enter the total area of the plot where the hotel or the lodging house is constructed.

b. From the Category drop-down list, select the category to which the hotel or the lodging house belongs.

 

NOTE: The hotels or the lodging houses are classified as either Posh, A Class or B Class.

 

c. In the Floor Details section, specify the details as mentioned in Table 4.

d. In the Star Hotels and Lodging House Details section, specify the following details:

§         Serial No: The serial number

§         Type: The type of rooms in the hotel. For example, single bed, double bed, non AC or AC rooms, suites. It depends on the hotel or the lodging houses.

§         No. of Units: The number of rooms available for each type.

 

NOTE: If a hotel has multiple type of rooms, click the Plus (+) icon and enter the details. Similarly, click the Cross (X) icon to delete the selected entry.

 

§         Rate Per Unit: The rate per each unit in rupees.

§         Total: The total amount in rupees. This field automatically gets filled in based on the rate per unit and the number of units.

                 e. In the Tax Effective Date field, enter the date (DD/MM/YYYY) effective from when the tax should be calculated.  According to the tax rules, tax cannot be raised with retrospect effect beyond 6 years.

 

NOTE: After you enter the tax effective date, the system automatically displays the installment starting from when you need to collect tax in the Demand Effective Installment field.

 

·      If you have selected Kalyana Mandapam, perform the following steps:

a. In the Area of Plot field, enter the total area of the plot where the Kalyana Mandapam is constructed.

b. From the Category drop-down list, select the category to which the Kalyana Mandapam belongs.

 

NOTE: The Kalyana Mandapams are classified as:

·      A Class with vessels 

·      A Class without vessels

·      B Class with vessels 

·      B Class without vessels

·      C Class with vessels  

·      C Class without vessels 

 

       c. In the Floor Details section, specify the details as mentioned in Table 4.

d. In the Rate Charged Per Marriage field, enter the rate (in rupees) charged for conducting a marriage in the Kalyana Mandapam.

e. In the Date of Completion/Occupation field, enter the date (DD/MM/YYYY) effective from when the tax should be calculated.  According to the tax rules, tax cannot be raised with retrospect effect beyond 6 years.

 

NOTE: After you enter the Date of Completion/Occupation, the system automatically displays the installment starting from when you need to collect tax in the Demand Effective Installment field.

 

 

NOTE: In the Comments field you can enter any description regarding the property.

 

9. Click Submit. You get the Create Property Confirmation page, which displays the details that you have entered.

10. Click one of the following:

·    Submit to confirm the details you have entered.

·    Back to modify the details you have entered.

 

Upon clicking Submit, you get an acknowledgement page with the following information:

·    Bill Number: You can use this number to track a property

·    Name of the owner

·    Address of the property

·    The half-yearly tax to be paid by the citizen

·    Order number

·    Order date

Along with the acknowledgement, you get the Notice No.6 with the property tax details. You can choose to print this notice.

 

NOTE:  When a property is registered, the tax is to be paid in two half-yearly installments. First half-yearly installment is from April to September and the second half-yearly installment is from October to March.

 

Table 4: Floor Details

Field

Description

Floor No

The number of the floor which is newly constructed. For example, if you have newly constructed the 3rd floor, you need to specify 3 in this field. For multiple floors, see the NOTE at the end of this table.

Build Up Area

The built-up area of the floor in square feet.

Usage

The new construction is used either for residential or commercial purpose.

Occupancy

The new construction is occupied by the owner or is given out for rent.

Type of Construction

Type of construction can be:

·    Permanent Construction: It refers to houses having fixed walls and roof.

·    Semipermanent Construction: It refers to houses having fixed or alterable walls and roofs.

Year of Construction

The year in which the floor was constructed.

Date of Completion/Occupation (This field is not applicable for government properties)

 

NOTE: After you enter the date of completion/occupation, the system automatically displays the installment in the Demand Effective Installment field and calculates the property tax demand based on the installment.

 

The date on which the construction was completed or when the owner occupied the premises, whichever comes earlier.

For example, if you occupy a house that is still under construction, then you need to enter the date when you occupy the house.

 

Service Charge Effective Date

 

NOTE: The service charge (if any) is calculated only against State or Central Government properties.

 

The date effective from when the service charge needs to be calculated.

 

NOTE: If the property has multiple floors, enter the respective floor details. Click the Plus (+) icon and specify the details of each floor. Similarly, click the Cross (X) icon to delete a floor.
