Introduction

 

The rules and requirements of a ULB may change from time to time.  Such changes must be reflected in the ERP modules for seamless operation of the ULB.

 

See the examples below from each product perspective:

 

·         eGov EIS: You may have credited leaves to your employees on a financial year basis previously, while you may be required to do it on a calender year basis from this year onwards.

 

·         eGov PTIS: You may have been charging a tax of Rs. 50 per square feet for vacant land till this year. From next year onwards, you may be required to change the tax rate to Rs. 60 per square feet.

 

·         eGov LCMS: You may be using an automatically-generated Legal Case Number to track a case. From this date onwards, you may need to have it generated manually.

 

·         eGov Works/Financials: you may have been using a manual workflow for approvals. Now, there is a new requirement where the approval workflows must happen automatically.

 

By using the configuration settings, you can set the master data values in tune with your changing business requirements.

 

Figure: Configuration Setup

 

Referring to the figure:

·         The first raw indicates that the leaves of ULB employees are calculated on a financial year basis. You can change the value of the Value field to "calenderBased" to make the leave calculation happen on a Calender Year basis.

·         The second raw indicates that holidays are not marked as "leave" when they fall within a leave period. You can change the value of the Value field to "Y" to mark the holidays as leaves.

·         The third raw indicates that the employees who create leave applications can approve them as well. You can change the value of the Value field to "N" if you do not want the employees who created the leave applications to approve them.
