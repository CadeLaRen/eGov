Mark Employee Attendance in Attendance Register

Employee attendance is tracked in the Attendance Register.

To access the Attendance Register for an employee or a department:

1. In the eGov EIS Payroll home page, click EIS-Payroll.

2. Click EIS.

3. Under EIS, click Attendance and then click Create.

4. In the Search Employee screen, specify the month for which you want to view the attendance.

5. Select one of the following:

  • To view and mark the attendance of a single employee, enter the employee code.
  • To view and mark the attendance of an entire department, click to select it from the drop-down list beside Department.

6. Click Submit. The Attendance Register based on the options you entered is displayed.

Mark Attendance

All authorised holidays in a month are shown in the Attendance Register with the letter H.

In the Attendance Register for a given month, the following can be entered:

  • Type P to mark the employee present on a given working day or holiday. If the employee is marked present on a holiday, and is eligible for compensatory off leave, a pop-up asking whether to grant a compensatory off holiday is displayed.
  • Type A to mark the employee absent on a given working day.
  • Type HP to indicate the employee was present on half the day.

After you have finished marking attendance, click Save.

NOTE: Notations L, NA, and C in the Attendance Register are generated by the eGov application. A user cannot enter them.

To indicate that an employee is on leave on a certain day, a leave application needs to be created and approved. After this is done, the letter L is displayed in the box corresponding to the date when the employee was on leave. For further details, see Apply for Leave and Create Leave Application.

When an employee works on a holiday and is awarded a compensatory off day, the attendance register for that day displays the letter C. See Compensatory Off.

When an employee joins the organisation after the first of the month, all the days before the joining date contain NA. This is system generated.
