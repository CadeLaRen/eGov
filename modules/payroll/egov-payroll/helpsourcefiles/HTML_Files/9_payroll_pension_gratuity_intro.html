About Gratuity

In a ULB, employees are eligible to receive a gratuity when they retire. The same gratuity amount is given to the employee's nominee if the employee dies before retirement.

The total payment that needs to be paid at the end of an employee's tenure (when the employee retires or is deceased) is:

Gratuity + one month's pension

Calculations for Computing Gratuity and Monthly Pension

Gratuity is calculated as the lower of:

  • Gratuity factor x Basic in employee's current salary

    and

  • Gratuity limit specified for the ULB

Pension that needs to be disbursed each month is calculated as:

Employee's current Basic + Employee's current DA

Commute Pension

Employees have an option to commute their pension (withdraw a part of their pension as a lump sum) at the time of retirement. When a pension is commuted, the employee can specify the new and reduced pension amount that they are willing to receive after withdrawing the lump sum.

The withdrawal amount, or the commute pension amount is calculated as:

Basic x Commute percentage x Commute period x 12

where,

Basic is the basic component in the employee's latest payslip.

Commute percentage is the percentage of the pension that needs to be commuted.

Commute period is the number of years for which an amount is deducted from the pension. Multiply the value by 12 (months) since the commute period is in years.

For an employee who has availed the commute pension option:

The total payment that needs to be made at the end of the employee's tenure is:

Gratuity + Commuted pension

The monthly pension that is payable to the employee is calculated as:

(Employee's current Basic + Employee's current DA) - (Basic x Commute percentage)

Suppose an employee whose Basic is Rs. 10,000 wants to commute 20% of his pension amount. If the ULB he works for defines the commute period as 10 years, the commute pension is calculated as follows:

10000 (Basic) x 20/100 (Commute percentage) x 10 (Commute period) x 12 = 240000

Hence Rs. 2,40,000 is the commuted pension amount.

If the employee's current DA is Rs.2500, the monthly pension payable to the employee for the next ten years is:

10000 (Basic) + 2500 (DA) - [10000 (Basic) x 20/100 (Commute percentage)] = Rs. 10500

NOTE: When the commute percentage is typed, all the gratuity calculations are done automatically in eGov EIS Payroll.

Calculate Pension and Gratuity

To calculate or view the gratuity and pension details for an employee, do the following:

1. Ensure the employee status is retired or deceased. See Update employee status to change the status.

2. In the EIS-Payroll menu, click Payroll.

3. Click Pension and then click Gratuity.

4. Do one of the following:

  • Click Create to create gratuity and pension details of an employee.
  • Click View to view gratuity and pension details of an employee.

5. In the Employee Code field, type the employee code of the employee whose gratuity details you need to calculate or view.

6. Do one of the following:

  • Click Compute Gratuity to calculate gratuity and pension details for the employee
  • Click View Gratuity to view the gratuity and pension details for an employee.
NOTE: If the pension details for the employee are not defined, the following error message is displayed:Pension header is not there for this employee. See Create Pension details to create pension details for the employee.

If the employee is deceased and there are no nominees to receive the pension and the gratuity, the following error message is displayed: This employee is deceased and there are no eligible nominees for this employee, so gratuity computation being terminated. See Create Pension details to create nominee details.
