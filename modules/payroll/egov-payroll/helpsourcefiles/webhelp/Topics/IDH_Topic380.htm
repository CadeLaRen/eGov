Introduction

 

Provident Fund is a fund to which both the employees and employer contribute at the rate of 12% of the basic wages, dearness allowance and retaining allowance, if any, payable to employees per month. It is returned to the employee at the time of his/her retirement.

Employees' Provident Fund Scheme takes care of following needs of the members:

·      Retirement

·      Medical Care

·      Family obligation

·      Education of Children

·      Financing of Insurance Polices

 

Maintaining employee Provident Fund details is a statutory requirement. This requires the following:

·      PF Liability Account--created and managed in the eGov Financials module

·      PF Interest Expense Account-created and managed in the eGov Financials module

 

Each month a Provident Fund deduction (PF Subscription) is made from the employee's salary and credited to the PF Liability Account using a batch process.

 

Interest is calculated on the amount in the PF Liability Account monthly or annually depending on what is specified. When an annual interest rate is specified, it is pro-rated for every month. Different interest rates can be indicated across a year based on prevailing market conditions. Interest is calculated on the last day of the month or the last day of the year based on how the PF Liability Account is set up.

 

For details on how to set up a PF Account, see Setting up PF.

Employee PF Accounts
