Introduction

 

When pension and gratuity are to be disbursed, the following situations are to be considered:

 

·      Employee is alive and retires: In this situation, the employee receives gratuity and pension.

·      Employee dies before retirement and eligible nominees are present: In this situation, the nominee(s) receives gratuity and pension.

·      Employee dies after retirement and eligible nominees are present: In this situation, the nominee(s) receive pension.

 

Consider the following two cases:

 

Case 1:Employee retires or dies before retirement

 

To disburse pension and gratuity when an employee retires or dies before retirement, do the following procedure:

 

1.   Update the employee status in the Employee Information System (EIS). For more information, see Updating Employee Status in EIS.

2.   Create a Pension record.

3.   Compute the total amount that needs to be disbursed. This is based on the following:

·      Pension amount

·      Gratuity or commuted gratuity as applicable

·      Unpaid loan amount

4.   Obtain approval from a superior official in the ULB.

5.   Record details for disbursal of pension.

6.   Disburse pension to the employee or the eligible nominees.

 

Case 2: Employee Dies After Retirement

 

When an employee dies after retirement and eligible nominees are present, such nominees continue to receive the total pension amount in proportionate shares. It must be remembered that the gratuity was paid at the time of retirement. For this reason, do the following procedure:

 

1.   Update the employee status in the Employee Information System (EIS). For more information, see Updating Employee Status in EIS.

2.   Update the Pension record to reflect nominees.

3.   Compute the pension amount to be disbursed to each of the eligible nominees.

NOTE: The total pension amount originally calculated is divided among the eligible nominees.
 

4.   Obtain approval from a superior official in the ULB.

5.   Disburse pension to the eligible nominees.
