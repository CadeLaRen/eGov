Introduction

 

The eGov Public Grievance Redressal (PGR) system is an application that enables the citizens of a city or town to register their grievances with the municipal corporation from the Internet, over the telephone, or in person. After the complaint is registered, a computer generated Complaint Number is given to the citizen. This can be used by the citizen to track and monitor the status of the complaint.

 

The eGov PGR System is also used by the city officials to register and process complaints.

 

Some of the salient features of PGR system are as follows:

·      Easy and systematic method to register complaints and track redressal.

·      Priorization of complaints.

·      Complaint forms are structured to enable better problem analysis and assignment.

·      Ability to lodge complaints from the Internet, by telephone,and for citizens to walk-in and write a complaint on paper.

·      Complaint number is generated for each complaint. The complainant can then track the complaint until it is resolved. Computerization and online tracking introduces transparency to the system.

·      Dynamic Management Information System (MIS) reports allow  government officials to  generate reports on the complaints by city, ward, street, department and so on. This helps to plan and prevent recurring problems in specific areas.

·      Automatic routing of complaints to appropriate officials.

·      Dynamic, up-to-date complaint statistics, accessible to the public, allowing for greater civic  awareness and citizen participation.

·      Timely communication between the Urban Local Bodies (ULB) officials and citizens.
