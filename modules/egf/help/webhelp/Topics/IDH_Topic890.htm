Introduction

 

The system treats a voucher as the basic unit of transaction. Each voucher is characterized by the following:

·         Each voucher must be a self-balancing entity – i.e. the sum of debits is equal to the sum of credits.

·         Each voucher must belong to a Fund (and hence should be reflected in the books of accounts of the Fund).

·         The chart of accounts attributes captured on a voucher (in addition to the Fund and Account codes) are determined based on the implementation specific configuration.

 

A voucher is created in Financials corresponding to each bill that is created. The bill creation can happen either in Financials or other modules. For example, Works department creates a contractor bill, Payroll department creates a salary bill, Stores department creates a supplier bill, and Legal Cell department creates an Expense bill.

 

Typically, an account assistant (or any officer designated by the ULB) creates a voucher for all bills. The vouchers will be forwarded to the Inbox of the designated officers for approval. The officers can verify that if the bill amount falls within the allocated budgeted amount for the department which created the bill.

 

There can be as many number of approval cycles as you want depending on your business requirements.

 

Each officer has the right to reject/approve the voucher at his/her discretion based on the adequacy of data provided. The rejected vouchers will be available in the Inbox of the officer who created the voucher for modifications, incorporating the changes suggested by the officer who rejected it.

 

A voucher is said to be "approved" only when all officers have approved it.

 

Once the voucher is in the "approved" state, the designated department ( e.g., Financial Management Unit) can make the payment through Bank Payment Voucher (BPV), which will also go through various approval cycles. There is a bank balance check that happens here to see if the payment made through a particular bank account has sufficient balance.

 

On approval of a voucher, the designated officer (e.g., account assistant) can assign a cheque against the payment.

 

The system maintains four types of vouchers:

·         Receipt Voucher

·         Payment Voucher

·         Contra Voucher

·         Journal Voucher

 

The different types of transactions are:

·      Receipts

·      Expenditure

 

The expenditure includes:

·      Procurement Orders

·      Salary, Contractor/Supplier, and Contingency Bill Payments

·      Contra Entries- Cash Deposition, Cash Withdrawal, and Bank-Bank Transfer

·      Journal Vouchers
