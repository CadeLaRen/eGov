Introduction

 

Every year, urban local bodies (ULBs) prepare a budget for the forthcoming financial year to ensure that their revenue is optimized while judiciously minimizing the spending.

 

Each department of the ULB will have its own budget and it will be prepared by the respective department head.

 

Budget is planned after identifying the sources of income and taking into account all current and future expenses.

 

Generally, ULBs plan a budget for the next financial year based on the actual revenue and expenses for the current and the previous financial years such as:

·      Last year actuals

·      Current year estimate

·      Actuals up to previous month end

·      Anticipatory up to 31 March

·      Revised Estimate proposed (current year)

·      Revised Estimate fixed (current year)

·      Budget Estimate Proposed (next year)

·      Budget Estimate fixed (next year)

 

The planning and preparation of a budget is time-consuming, cumbersome, and involves significant effort by a team of people.

 

The Budgeting module is exclusively designed and developed for ULBs to help them manage the complete lifecycle of a budget.

 

The four stages of a budget lifecycle are:

·      Setting Up a Budget Definition

·      Setting Up a Budget Group

·      Setting Up Budget Details

·      Approving a Budget

 

NOTE: List of attributes (level of detail) at which budget data is managed varies from one implementation to another.

 

 

Revised Estimate (RE)

 

Because the budget is subject to change based on economic and unexpected circumstances, you may need to revise the proposed amount of a budget when you operate in the financial year for which the budget was defined. Such budgets are called revised estimates.

 

For example, assume that you have defined a budget for the Engineering department to undertake the construction of a flyover in 2010-11. The proposed amount is 15 crores. When the Engineering department started the work in the 2010-11 financial year, they realize that they cannot execute the work with the proposed amount because of the substantial increase in steel and cement prices. In such a situation, you can revise the proposed amount to a higher value.
