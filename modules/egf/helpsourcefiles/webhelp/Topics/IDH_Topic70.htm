Introduction

 

After creating a budget definition, you need to figure out the list of anticipated financial transactions that may occur in the specific department for the financial year for which the budget was defined.

 

These transactions will come under the following categories:

·      Revenue Receipts

·      Revenue Expenditure

·      Capital Receipts

·      Capital Expenditure

 

For example, you have created a budget for your department, say Department1Budget. Assume that Department1 is anticipating the following financial transactions for the next financial year:

·      Building a shopping complex

·      Collecting the rent of buildings owned by the department

·      Paying salary to employees who belong to the department

 

In this case, you need to create a budget group for each of the transactions above and map each budget group to the corresponding account code.

 

Setting up a budget group includes the following actions:

·      Creating a budget Group

·      Modifying a budget Group

·      Viewing a budget Group
