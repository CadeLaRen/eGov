Closing a Period

 

Period closing is an activity that must be performed to close a period or a financial year. Using this feature, you can:

·         Close a Financial Year and Transfer a Closing Balance

·         Close a Financial Year only

·         Transfer a Closing Balance Only

·         Close a date range for posting

·         Re-open a date range for posting

 

To close a period, perform the following steps:

1. Click Period-End Activities > Close Period to display the Fiscal Period Setup page.

2. Click one of the following options:

·             Close financial Year: To close a financial Year

·             Re-open Date Range:To re-open a date range that was soft-closed.

·             Close Date Range:  To hard/soft-close a date range

·             Transfer closing Balance: To transfer closing balances

NOTE: Once a Financial Year is closed, the system does not allow any postings for that year.

On closing a Financial Year, the following need to be selected: Transfer Closing Balances and Close Financial Year.

1.           The following activities are performed on Closing the Financial Year:

2.           The net of income over expenditure (calculated as the net balance of all the income and expenditure accounts as of the last day of the financial year) is transferred to the Municipal Fund Account (identified by from the Chart of Accounts where Purpose = ‘Excess IE’)

3.           The closing balances of all the Asset and Liability Accounts are transferred to the Opening Balance of the following year.

4.           The current financial year is closed – and CANNOT be opened again for posting of transactions.

 

The system computes the closing balances as follows:

 

Income/Expenditure Accounts

                    For each income account (Type=1), the net balance is computed as follows:

Closing balance = Opening credit balance + Sum of credit transactions – (Sum of debit transactions + Opening debit balance)

For each expenditure account (Type=2), the net balance is computed as follows:

Closing balance = Opening debit balance + Sum of debit transactions – (sum of credit transactions + Opening credit balance)

          The net balance (excess of income over expenditure) is computed once the above is done:

If the balance is greater than zero, the amount is added to the CREDIT BALANCE of the liability account that is assigned a purpose code of 7 (‘Excess of Income over Expenditure’)

          If the balance is less than zero, the amount is added to the DEBIT BALANCE of the liability account that is assigned a purpose code of 7 (‘Excess of Income over Expenditure’)

 

          Only one liability detailed account code MUST be designated with purpose code of 7. The net balance is updated as the opening balance of the NEXT FINANCIAL YEAR for that liability account code.

 

Asset/Liability accounts

          For each liability account (Type=3), the net balance is computed as follows:

          Closing balance = Opening credit balance + Sum of credit transactions – (Sum of debit transactions + Opening debit balance)

          For each asset account (Type=4), the net balance is computed as follows:

Closing balance = Opening debit balance + Sum of debit transactions – (sum of credit transactions + Opening credit balance)

 

For each asset/liability account, the net balance is updated as the opening balance of the NEXT FINANCIAL YEAR for that account code.
