Introduction

 

Urban Local Bodies (ULBs) of India are the constitutionally provided administrative units that provide basic infrastructure and services in cities and towns. Large urban areas are governed by nagar nigams, often simply called corporations. Smaller urban areas are governed by nagar palika, which are often referred to simply as municipalities.

 

For their day-to-day functioning, ULBs are involved in a range of financial transactions. Typically incomes are in the form of taxes, fees, fines and grants; expenses are in the form of payments to suppliers, contractors, salary bills and other miscellaneous expenses.

 

ULBs raise funds from the Central Government, from banks, and financial institutions to meet their financial requirements.

 

Some of the responsibilities of the ULB accounting department are as follows:

 

·      Financials and fixed assets reporting

·      Maintain account payables

·      Maintain the general and subsidiary ledgers

·      Record all revenues

·      Prepare the general purpose financial statements

·      Prepare budgets

 

Manually maintaining all financial transactions in a ULB is a tedious process with a greater probability of human error and higher chances of fraud.

 

eGov Financials is an application that automates the end-to-end financial operations of a ULB by providing an integrated system that manages budgeting, asset management, in addition to the daily accounting and reporting. This system is specifically built for Municipal Accounting and its particular needs based on the National Municipal Account Manual (NMAM) published by Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG) and Ministry of Urban Development, Government of India.
